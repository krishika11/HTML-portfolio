Forms of linear equations

What is the slope and y intercept? How do you calculate slope?

In math, slope is the steepness of a specific line in the direction it goes. Slope is also commonly known as rate of change. The steps below show you how to find the slope of a line.

  1. Find 2 points in the line.
  2. Find the Y coordinate and X coordinate of that line.
  3. Subtract the 2 Y coordinates.
  4. Subtract the 2 X coordinates.
  5. Divide them.
Y intercepts are when the line intersects the y-axis. Y intercepts are when the x coordinate is equal to 0.

Slope-intercept form

Slope-intercept is in the form of y=mx+b, where m is the slope and b is y intercept. It is really easy to interpret the slope and y intercept when a lines equation is slope-intercept form.

In the equation, y=7x+8, the slope would be 7 and the y intercept would be 8. Slope-intercept form directly shows you the slope and y intercept. Here are a few practice problems.

Find the slope and y intercept.
  • Y=12x+15
  • Y=2x+6
  • Y=9x+10
  • Y=4x+5

Point-slope form

The formula for point-slope form is y-y1=m(x-x1), where y1 and x1 are the x and y coordinates and m is the slope. As you can see, this form is not as clear as slope-intercept but it's still very beneficial to learn. Unlike slope-intercept, this doesnt directly show the y intercept, it makes us work to see the y intercept. We have to convert it into slope intercept to see the intercept. The steps listed below help you find the y-intercept.

  1. Use the distributive property of multiplication to get rid of the parentheses
  2. Add or subtract(inverse of the sign written) y1 to x1
    3. That's it

    In the equation y-6=3(x-1).The slope would obviously be 3. To convert it into slope intercept form, you have to distribute. Using the distributive property the new equation would be y-6=3x-3. Next, I will add 6 to -3. Now, the slope intercept version is y=3x+3 The Y intercept is 3.

    Find the slope and y intercept.
    • y-3=3(x+2)
    • y+6=5(x+4)
    • y+1=6(x+5)

    Standard form

    Last but definitely not least, standard form. The formula for standard form is Ax+By=C. In this formula, we can't see the slope or the y intercept clearly (of course). Like point slope form, we have to convert it into slope-intercept form. Belw are the steps to convert it into slope-intercept form.

    1. Add or subtract Ax from both sides
    2. divide both sides by B to isolate Y
    Thats it!
    For example, if I had the equation 6x+3y=3, I would first subtract 6x from both sides. My new equation would be 3y=3-6x.Lastly, I would divide by 3 on both sides. Now, my equation has been succesfully converted into slope intercept, making it y=1-2x. My slope would be -2 and my y intercept would be 1.
